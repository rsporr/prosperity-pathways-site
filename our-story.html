How it started

Prosperity Pathways began with a single exam fee paid out of pocket. A family member witnessed a student — a strong, capable kid — unable to sit for an AP exam he had prepared for because the cost was out of reach. The family stepped in and covered it directly.

From that moment, the idea took root. Over the following year, the family quietly funded exam fees for a small group of students at a local school, spending their own money with no formal structure behind it. Then life moved on, and the effort paused.

Years later, River Sporrer and Ibrahim Salih brought it back — this time with a real foundation behind it. They registered Prosperity Pathways AP Foundation as a 501(c)(3), built a referral process with partner schools, and scaled what had started as a family act of generosity into an organization that could reach students across multiple schools. What began informally, in a family, is now run by students — for students.
